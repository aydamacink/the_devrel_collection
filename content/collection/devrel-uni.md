## Why I chose to Highlight It

“You should apply”, A name kept surfacing. I had already looked it up myself, but people continued to tell me it was the best I could do in DMs, calls and casual conversations. I met the founder. I was sold, but last year there were no cohorts open: I waited, I applied, I got in and it didn’t disappoint.

DevRel Uni is a 5-week cohort-based program of 5 Sessions and 5 mentors. This year the theme was : What does it mean to build, teach, and learn now that we are in the AI era?. Bianca Buzea (Founder and Head of DevRel at Chronicle Labs) opened it, Nader Dabit (cognition), Patrick Skinner(Superbuilders), Hassan El Mghari (Together AI), and Francesco Andreoli (Consensys) each gave a session.

Hassan’s session shifted my thinking. His point, also one making the rounds more widely: AI has made building almost free, but now taste is the true differentiator. I noticed how this sat in my body. Part of me agreed, but part of me questioned this taste : Who defines it and sets the rules? My Project was on payments, but was it any good, did it have taste? And to whose standards? I closed my payments project that week. The question that Hassan left lingering in my head was more interesting than the project itself.
Previously we had Nader’s session who talked about the value of being able to adapt and change quickly without overidentifying with a stack (or project). Patrick had also talked about systems that compound over time, and Bianca about the three audiences we now have : developers, AI assisted non-technical builders, and agents. Francesco, in the final session spoke about the importance of translating to terms that people outside your team will understand, and that goes back to my question: What is taste, and what does it mean for different people?

I decided to explore devrel artifacts, docs, objects, videos, essays and collect the ones I think will still be valuable. Even after AI. I allowed my project to change, and it became more interesting , and personal to me. Where do I see taste? What do I value? Having it in a project will systematize it for me. As I select the pieces, I think about Bianca’s question and the three audiences. I also avoid jargon, and try to explain in simple terms what “taste” is for me, and the “quality gate” I was running.

I am excited to continue with my project, searching for those jewels -little wooden horses- that will capture my attention.
