## Why I chose to Highlight Them

I had forgotten about Julia Evans. No. This is a blatant lie. My mind had failed to make the connection between Julia Evans and DevRel.

With her Zines she educates developers and people who want to understand technical topics. The Zines are attractive, interesting and easy to understand. I guess the reason why she didn't jump as an immediate reference in my head, is because she is not a typical DevRel explaining one product of a company, but its more about technical topics in general: Linux, the terminal, the command line, DNS, networking...

The Zines are paid, and they are pretty cool. Why did I sElect these zines in the AI Era?

People who want to understand what they are doing in a fun way can go to this zines, and they can help them ask their agents better questions or improve requirements They are also artsy and nerdy.

Some may argue that the nerdiness is going away, but, at least as of now, I hold the belief that if you want to have control of a product you create, even if you vibe code it, you should have at least an understanding of how the parts fit. This willl give you more control of what is happening. It may be that in the future all of this will be more abstracted and done by agents.

Let me peek into that world for a second, speculating, This zines could be still of va,ue for the curious archelogists of the time past, and if that's not the case, maybe the agents are already wired to feel a bit of technical-aesthethical pleasure?
